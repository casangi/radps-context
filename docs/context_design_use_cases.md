Use Case Template

Adapted from “Use Case Modeling” by Kurt Bittner and Ian Spence.

Aim for less than 3 pages per use case. The goal here is to capture the major features of the individual use case, not every single detail. Additional details will be captured later in the design phase.

Use Case <number>: <title>

    Relevant Stakeholders
        The relevant stakeholders for this use case.
    Frequency:
        How frequently (e.g., high/medium/low) the stakeholder group anticipates encountering the use case
    Importance:
        How important (e.g., high/medium/low) the report is to that stakeholder group for achieving their objectives
    Actors:
        Actors are entities that interact with the system. They can be users, other systems, or hardware devices. For example, in an online shopping system, actors could include customers and administrators.
    Goals:
        Goals represent the outcomes of an actor's interactions with the system. For instance, a customer's goal in an online shopping system might be to purchase a product.
        Include relevant context as necessary.
    Preconditions:
        Preconditions are conditions that must be true before the use case can be executed. For example, a precondition for a customer to add a product to the cart might be that the customer is logged in.
    Basic Flow:
        The basic flow describes the main success scenario where everything works as intended. For example, a customer searches for a product, adds it to the cart, and completes the checkout process.
    Alternative Flows (optional):
        Alternative flows describe deviations from the main success scenario, such as errors or exceptions. For example, if a product is out of stock, the customer might receive a notification
